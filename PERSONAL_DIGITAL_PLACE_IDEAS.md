# Personal Digital Place Ideas

This file is for ideas I do not want to lose while reshaping the site from a portfolio into a personal digital place.

## A Personal Museum

A museum could be made from ordinary things, not only achievements.

Possible collections:

- Things my mother keeps
- Objects on my desk during burnout
- The last 5 meals I ate alone
- Receipts from a week of being alive
- Screenshots I never deleted

Each object gets:

- a title
- a caption
- an emotional label

Friends can visit and leave one museum note.

Why this matters:

This could make the site feel like a personal digital wall: not a resume, not a normal portfolio, but a place where small evidence of living, thinking, and becoming can stay visible.

## Living Field Notes

Not just a digital zoo.

It becomes a personal living field notebook.

A website where each animal is part educational, part personal memory, and part playful creature.

Core idea:

Living Field Notes is a personal 2D digital zoo where animals I research become interactive creatures with facts, moods, behaviors, and my own notes.

What makes it unique:

- why I chose that animal
- what I learned about it
- what surprised me
- my favorite breeds or species
- my drawings or style choices
- notes from research
- small opinions like "I think this one looks dramatic"
- personal rankings
- small stories
- my own categories

Example animal page:

Cat

- What I learned
- My favorite cat breeds
- Weird facts I did not know before
- My personal notes
- Why I like cats
- Cat behavior I find interesting
- Mini quiz
- Mood: curious / sleepy / chaotic / affectionate
- In the field: cats wander, sit, stretch, chase light, and ignore the user sometimes

Possible sections:

- Field View: animals walk around in a 2D open field
- My Zoo Notes: a personal journal-style section for each animal
- Animal Cards: facts plus personal notes
- Favorites Shelf: favorite animals, breeds, and facts
- Research Log: "Today I learned..." entries
- Creature Personality: each animal has a mood and behavior inspired by research

Possible names:

- My Digital Zoo
- Kaung's Creature Field
- My Animal Notebook
- Little Living Zoo
- Field Notes Zoo
- The Animal Field
- Pocket Habitat
- My Wild Notes
- Creature Journal
- Living Field Notes

Why this matters:

This gives the site permission to be both educational and personal. It can teach people, but still feel like it came from me instead of from a textbook.

## Editorial Design Pull

I personally feel pulled toward editorial design.

The site should not depend on a loud fixed brand palette. It can have a plain, calm background, and the color can come from the content itself: blog posts, thoughts, books, drawings, animal notes, project images, personal objects, and whatever I place on the site.

Design principle:

- the background stays quiet
- the layout feels editorial
- typography and spacing carry the identity
- color appears through content, not decoration
- each section can borrow its mood from what it contains
- the site should feel like a thoughtful publication or personal magazine, not a generic portfolio

Why this matters:

This may be why editorial design feels close to me. It gives structure without forcing one fixed mood. It lets my thinking, writing, books, projects, notes, and memories bring their own color.
